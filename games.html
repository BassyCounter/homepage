Some of my favorite games:


Pokémon

Pokémon was the first game that I was ever introduced to. I don't remember exactly how old I was, but I was in elementary school, probably in 1st or 2nd grade. I know it was the first game I was ever introduced to because I was gifted a purple GameBoy Advance with a few games from my dad on Christmas, one of them being a copy of Pokémon Silver. Now the thing about Pokémon Silver was that the game was made for the GameBoy Color systems, and those cartridges were quite noticeably longer than the cartridges built for the GameBoy Advance systems. I think that played a huge factor into why it was the first game I played because all of the other games I were gifted were on GameBoy Advance cartridges. On top of the size factor, the color of the cartridge was a light gray color as opposed to the dark gray, charcoal looking colors of the cartridges for GameBoy Advance, and the label for Pokémon Silver featured, what I thought of as, really cool artwork of a character, presumably from the game itself. I later learned that it was artwork of Lugia, the primary legendary Pokémon of the game.

The first Pokémon I chose however was Totodile. When I was tasked with choosing my first Pokémon, the one with my favorite design at the time was Totodile. Usually I go with the fire starter at the beginning of Pokémon games just because they usually are the strongest of the three and result in better outcomes in battles, but this was my first Pokémon experience, and I was also just a little kid. I wasn't very aware of how battles and all that worked or anything at that point in time. In fact, I don't think I ever beat the Elite Four with that save. Unfortunately, I gave away my GameBoy and my games when I was in middle school (a decision I still regret to this day).

Living Dex

Throughout the years since I first played Pokémon, I would go back to it here and there just because it gave me some comfort with how simple and fun it could be. I've played at least one version of each generation of Pokémon, mainly because certain Pokémon can only be collected within a particular generation, and one of the goals I set out to achieve a couple years ago was to catch them all. Within the Pokémon collecting community exists a term called a Living Dex, which is essentially a collection of every Pokémon species, including each stage of evolution they exist in. Now on the surface, this doesn't seem like much a challenge, you would just catch every Pokémon you come across and then eventually you would have them all, right? Well... not exactly. You see, some Pokémon are mythicals, which are a species of Pokémon that are incredibly rare and aren't necessarily available within the games of that generation they were introduced in. A lot of them required you to go to specific locations in the real world and have them loaded onto your save of the game, and these were time-limited events. Other mythicals were only conceptual until YEARS later when they were released during some sort of event, much like the previously explained ones, or they were distributed online through what are called Mystery Gifts, which you can only claim one of (per save), and may or may not expire. All mythicals are considered rare, with some of them being always available, but only until you reach a milestone within one of the games, usually requiring you to beat the Elite Four (effectively beating the game) and/or completing the Pokémon entries within your Pokédex for every non-mythical of that generation. It's safe to say that if you acquired a mythical yourself, you put in a lot of effort. Even non-mythical Pokémon can be hard to acquire, ones that require trades to evolve, or have a incredibly low probability of spawning. Legendary Pokémon can typically only be caught once per save, and require the player to have completed most, if not all, of the game, and have a really low catch rate. The challenge of completing a Living Dex is not an easy feat, especially since new Pokémon generations are still being introduced every few years. However with all of that said, my Living Dex is looking pretty good! I'm only missing 2 mythicals, one of which is set to reappear as an event for Pokémon Go players in about a week and won't expire, so as long as the player works to finish the event (and hasn't already gotten the mythical within Pokémon Go), they're guaranteed an encounter with the mythical, and from what I can remember, the Pokémon can never runaway with events like this, so really the only challenge is the catch rate. Aside from the 2 mythicals, I have generations 1 through 8 complete, and I have most of generation 9 complete as well, I just haven't purchased the DLC that unlocks some of the new Pokémon that were released after the games were. There's a few paradox Pokémon I'm missing as well, but those ones were also released after the games were, and require an online membership for the Nintendo Switch to participate in the online raid events. I haven't been actively playing Pokémon for about a year, so I haven't been participating in those new events or paying for my Pokémon Home subscription (which is where my collection lives). I do intend on coming back to it at some point and updating my Pokémon Home Pokédex entries and finish collecting the remaining Pokémon that I have left.

Pokémon Go

While I do prefer the gameplay of the main series Pokémon games, Pokémon Go is really good at making the aspect of playing with friends extremely fun because it requires you be with each other in the real world and go on adventures together to catch new Pokémon and battle gyms to earn rewards. It's a great game for people trying to get into the habit of exercising. You can go for a walk, ride your bike, or just drive around (as long as you're careful) and catch Pokémon in the process. I'm on Team Valor, what about you?



Minecraft

My favorite game of all time is Minecraft. I have spent so many hours playing Minecraft, and I still have just as much fun playing it as when I played it for the first time. The only difference is that I have a lot more knowledge about the game than when I first started. Everything about the game I find really fascinating, from the abiiity to create/play mods, to being able to express yourself creatively, it's all super appealing to me. Nowadays, I mostly play in survival and like to build up bases that provide a lot of functionality, as well as look really cool and aesthetically pleasing. Even the simple things like mining and chopping down wood, I find very relaxing and it helps me to decompress after a long day. I'm not that great with using redstone, but the things that people have created with it is really inspiring. People have built flying machines, which are basically airplanes that are restricted to move at a certain level horizontally or vertically. Other inventions, include TNT launchers, binary encoders, and farms specifically created to automate the process of collecting certain materials. I find binary encoders particularly fascinating because they make it possible to create computers in Minecraft, as well as other machines that rely on the use of specific signals to achieve a certain task. The ability to use a sandbox world like the ones in Minecraft allow for the creation of so many unique things that you can share with friends or family, and create memories with. I don't think there will ever come a day where I stop liking the game.
