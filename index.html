About me:

Hello world! My name is Michael, and welcome to my homepage! I have a little bit of prior experience with HTML and CSS, but not so much experience with JavaScript and so hopefully this homepage results in something that helps me practice what I've learned so far, as well as put some new things such as JavaScript and Bootstrap into practice!

With that in mind, let's get on with some stuff about me! Up until around the end of 2022, I genuinely had no clue as to what I wanted to do career-wise. I knew that I wanted to pursue a career path that I could use my brain to resolve problems with, rather than my body, but that's pretty much the only thing I was sure of. Growing up, I didn't really struggle with class work all that much, in fact I never really remember needing to study before tests or refresh my brain on certain topics until I attempted college. I knew that I wasn't uncapable of doing something if I truly pushed myself to pursue it. It's pretty common for people to study to pass a class, so by that logic, it's probably pretty uncommon that I never really had to do that most of my life. I took a few advanced classes too while working towards by high school diploma, so looking back on that, I felt sure that I had potential for something more. What that 'something more' was/is, I'm still not too sure, but I'm hoping my journey with Computer Science and Programming gets me closer to that end goal. You can can check out my webpage here for my journey so far.

Computer Science/Programming

When it comes to computer related stuff, or really anything that's an electronic and uses a mixture of hardware and software, I've always had a fascination with. While I dabbled with a very small amount of HTML while I was in middle school/high school and began to scratch the surface of programming fundamentals with Scratch in high school (pun very much intended), I never really bothered to try to dive in and give it my best effort to learn how to program, what technologies did what, and how they work together until roughly 14 months ago. As far as how things work on the hardware side, I'm still pretty much just as clueless as I've ever been, though eventually I do wish to dive more into Robotics/Electrical Enginneering concepts at some point. Ever since taking the plunge, I immediately fell in love with programming. After learning some of the fundamentals, it opened my mind to how versatile and useful programming can be. In today's world, there's not a single thing it seems that cannot benefit from the use of programming. Anywhere from creating a website to be usable from anywhere in the world, to creating programs that automate simple tasks and make systems more efficient, the possibilities are nearly limitless. Music

Music has been a huge interest of mine ever since around 6th grade. I remember distinctly the first song that sparked my interest was Paradise by Coldplay. I'm not sure quite what it was, but at that particular moment in time, that song fired a unique set of neurons that made me feel something about music that no song had ever made me feel before. Every since then, music became very closely tied to my identity. I would spend almost all of my free time trying to find more music that gave me the same kind of rush. My music taste has changed drastically since hearing Paradise for the first time, but that was a monumental moment for me that probably changed the trajectory of my future by quite a bit. 6th grade was also the first year that I was old enough to join band. I originally wanted to play French Horn, but I couldn't manage to blow through the mouthpiece, so I had to settle for playing the baritone. It wasn't my ideal choice of instrument, but I did stay first chair most of the time, and even got an award at the end of the year for Best Brass Player (of my school and grade). I didn't continue with band in the years following, but I did eventually find my way to learning guitar. I became quite interested in music theory along the way as well. The fact that there's words and formulas that are behind the scenes that explain why music sounds good is incredibly fascinating to me. Because music made such an impact on my life, my first programming project ever was actually a music theory guide of sorts. You can find it here. If you would like to check out some of my favorite albums, you can check out this page.
